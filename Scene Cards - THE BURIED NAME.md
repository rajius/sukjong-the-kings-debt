# Scene Cards - THE BURIED NAME

## How to Use This

Each card is meant to force the chapter to behave like a chapter, not an idea dump.

Every card answers four questions:

- What does the chapter want?
- What resists that want?
- What changes irreversibly?
- What image should the reader leave with?

If a drafted chapter cannot answer these clearly, it is still outline material.

## Chapter 1 - The Boy Who Inherited a Crime

POV:

- Sukjong

Objective:

- Receive the petition on Nosan-gun and choose a response that reveals both caution and intent.

Conflict:

- Full restoration is politically dangerous.
- Doing nothing confirms inherited cowardice.
- Advisers or ministers push him toward safety, precedent, and silence.

Turn:

- Sukjong chooses the half-measure: "Nosan Daegun."
- He does not restore Danjong, but he moves the stone.

Ending image:

- Petition in his hand, decision made, but not enough. The state has shifted by one word.

## Interstitial 1 - The Body

POV:

- Third-person, distant

Objective:

- Establish the original act: someone is lifting a dead king from the earth.

Conflict:

- Cold, secrecy, fear, physical burden

Turn:

- The body is moved.

Ending image:

- Weight in the dark, no names, only consequence.

## Chapter 2 - The Teacher's Student

POV:

- The Widow

Objective:

- Establish her mind, her training, her marriage, and the hidden current beneath ordinary life.

Conflict:

- She is intelligent but structurally excluded.
- The teacher and her husband share knowledge she only partly sees.
- Stability depends on not asking certain questions.

Turn:

- She notices that the men around her are serving a purpose larger than household life.

Ending image:

- A lesson, a marriage, a withheld conversation just beyond a doorway.

## Chapter 3 - The Murder

POV:

- The Widow

Objective:

- Turn her from participant in someone else's cause into owner of the wound.

Conflict:

- Her husband's death is meant to look ordinary.
- Witnesses are quiet.
- The teacher is dying and cannot fully explain.

Turn:

- She inherits the teacher's papers and one concrete clue that points beyond the province.

Ending image:

- A letter, seal, or fragment in her hand that should not exist.

## Chapter 4 - The Correspondence Web

POV:

- The Widow

Objective:

- Show how she turns the teacher's scholarly network into a covert investigative instrument.

Conflict:

- She has to impersonate authority she does not officially possess.
- Male networks are useful but not safe.
- One wrong letter could expose the fiction.

Turn:

- Two separate lines of inquiry converge on the same protected name.

Ending image:

- Ink drying on a forged continuation of a dead man's voice.

## Chapter 5 - The Altar Not Yet Built

POV:

- Sukjong

Objective:

- Let Sukjong develop the private ideological frame that may later justify action.

Conflict:

- He sees the moral parallel he wants, but cannot yet spend it publicly.
- Acting before conditions ripen could destroy the project.

Turn:

- Sukjong privately adopts the Wanli/Jianwen analogy as a usable internal model.

Ending image:

- A king filing away an argument he cannot yet speak.

## Interstitial 2 - Preparation

POV:

- Third-person, distant

Objective:

- Show Eom Heung-do preparing to bury the dead in secret.

Conflict:

- Surveillance, household fear, practical danger

Turn:

- The burial becomes inevitable once the materials are assembled.

Ending image:

- His wife in the doorway, asking nothing.

## Chapter 6 - The Queen's Fall

POV:

- Sukjong primary, Widow secondary by consequence

Objective:

- Show the 1689 hwanguk as machinery, not merely event.

Conflict:

- Sukjong consolidates through destruction.
- The Widow's network is suddenly exposed to purge logic.
- Contacts disappear not because they failed her, but because power changed temperature.

Turn:

- Her capital source sends a final panicked warning tying her target to purge politics, then vanishes.

Ending image:

- A last letter arriving from a world already closing.

## Chapter 7 - The Queen Restored

POV:

- Sukjong

Objective:

- Show that reversal is not repair, and that stabilization is morally stained.

Conflict:

- Sukjong gets the political ground he needs.
- He also sees that the system restores positions more easily than lives.

Turn:

- The country becomes governable enough for Danjong's restoration to become possible.

Ending image:

- Order returning over wreckage that remains where it fell.

## Chapter 8 - The Inspector

POV:

- The amhaeng-eosa

Objective:

- Introduce the king's eyes on the ground as a person, not a function.

Conflict:

- He carries royal authority but must travel as if powerless.
- He believes in service, but the road shows him what policy becomes outside Seoul.

Turn:

- Before reaching Yeongwol, he encounters evidence that moral repair will travel through socially damaged ground.

Ending image:

- A disguised inspector realizing that orders arrive in places already full of ghosts.

## Chapter 9 - The Road

POV:

- The amhaeng-eosa and the Widow

Objective:

- Create narrative chemistry through mutual testing.

Conflict:

- He needs intelligence but cannot trust her.
- She sees what he is and does not want to be used.
- Their goals overlap without matching.

Turn:

- They agree to exchange information while withholding judgment.

Ending image:

- Two travelers moving in parallel, neither ahead of the other.

## Chapter 10 - The Petition

POV:

- Sukjong with the amhaeng-eosa's reports as structural pressure

Objective:

- Turn local findings into public royal action.

Conflict:

- Court objections invoke precedent, dynastic stability, and the danger of reopening Sejo.
- Sukjong must win without pretending the objections are trivial.
- The reports from the ground should not be wholly clean; there should be tension inside the victory.

Turn:

- Sukjong forces the debate across the line and commits the state to restoration.

Ending image:

- The argument turns, but at visible political cost.

## Interstitial 3 - The Ascent

POV:

- Third-person, distant

Objective:

- Show the physical labor of carrying the body uphill.

Conflict:

- Snow, terrain, exhaustion, discovery

Turn:

- The grave site is reached.

Ending image:

- The pine tree witnessing what the state refuses to witness.

## Chapter 11 - The Name

POV:

- All three threads by controlled convergence

Objective:

- Deliver the restoration as public fact and private destabilization.

Conflict:

- Sukjong gets the decree.
- The amhaeng-eosa must witness its reception honestly, not ceremonially.
- The Widow feels release before she fully understands what has been released.

Turn:

- Danjong is restored, the mission seems complete, and the Widow's guard lowers enough for attachment to become possible.

Ending image:

- Exhaustion, not celebration, settling over Yeongwol.

## Chapter 12 - The Altar

POV:

- Sukjong

Objective:

- Link Daebodan to Sukjong's need to monumentalize moral rule.

Conflict:

- Public ritual can fix memory, not motive.
- He has acted, but still cannot know whether he acted when right or when convenient.

Turn:

- The altar is built, and instead of closure, Sukjong gets self-accusation in stone form.

Ending image:

- Ceremony complete, doubt intact.

## Chapter 13 - The Name Behind the Name

POV:

- The Widow and the amhaeng-eosa

Objective:

- Reveal that the husband's death belongs inside the same political victory the novel has just achieved.

Conflict:

- She assembles the truth piece by piece.
- He recognizes the king's implication from the opposite side.
- Their brief emotional opening cannot survive what the king's seal now means.

Turn:

- She withdraws without accusation, and the relationship becomes impossible.

Ending image:

- A door closing without being slammed.

## Chapter 14 - Changjeolsa

POV:

- Convergent, restrained

Objective:

- End the novel in moral suspension rather than emotional settlement.

Conflict:

- The cause is won.
- The dead remain dead.
- Justice and complicity stand in the same shrine.

Turn:

- No new plot turn. The final change is condition: both are present before the same memory, and neither can simplify it.

Ending image:

- Two people in winter light at Yeongwol, separated by history, duty, grief, and the possibility that separation is not final.

## Final Interstitial - The Finished Grave

POV:

- Third-person, distant

Objective:

- Complete the burial and place all later history against an act that expected no acknowledgment.

Conflict:

- None beyond completion itself. This is aftermath stripped of recognition.

Turn:

- The work is done, and Eom Heung-do disappears back into anonymity.

Ending image:

- Snow covering disturbed earth by morning.

## Structural Checks

Use these tests while drafting.

For Sukjong chapters:

- Does he decide something?
- Does that decision cost him something?
- Is the chapter about more than his intelligence?

For Widow chapters:

- Does she gain, lose, or misread something concrete?
- Does the network feel difficult, not merely elegant?
- Is grief doing plot work rather than only tonal work?

For amhaeng-eosa chapters:

- Is he more than a courier?
- Does he discover the difference between royal language and provincial reality?
- Is his bond with the Widow built through pressure rather than softness alone?

For every ending:

- Has the chapter changed the terms of the next one?
- Is the final image stronger than the final explanation?

## Recommended Drafting Order

If you draft out of sequence, do it in this order:

1. Chapter 3
2. Chapter 1
3. Chapter 9
4. Chapter 10
5. Chapter 13
6. Chapter 14
7. Chapter 11
8. Chapter 8
9. Chapter 6
10. Chapter 7
11. Chapter 4
12. Chapter 2
13. Chapter 5
14. Chapter 12
15. Interstitials

Reason:

- this order locks the novel's main engines first: wound, state action, meeting, revelation, ending
- it reduces the risk of writing elegant scaffolding before the live material exists
