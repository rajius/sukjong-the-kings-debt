# THE DEAD MAN

*Placement: 1695, second meeting. Yi Hwon tracks Lady Yoon's network and finds her eliminating a compromised contact. Both covers blown through deadly confrontation and mutual recognition.*

---

The man was already dead when Yi Hwon stepped through the door.

A woman knelt beside the body, her face covered by a dark veil, her hands moving through the dead man's belongings. She did not startle when he entered. Simply continued her work while tracking his movement.

He took another step into the room, testing.

She moved. One moment crouched by the body, the next she had him locked, his arm twisted, his throat caught in the crook of her arm in a position where struggle would destroy them both.

He could have broken the hold, perhaps, but not without cost. Better to wait.

During the brief struggle, her veil slipped, falling away from her face.

Yi Hwon went very still.

So did she. For a moment neither of them moved. The memorial returned all at once. Then something changed between them — in his breathing, in her own body answering it — and she stepped back at once.

"Turn around."

He turned. She stood near the far wall, hands empty.

"Royal inspector."

"Network operative."

The dead man lay between them.

"Compromised," she said.

"By whom?"

"Others. There's a coordinated effort."

She moved toward the door. "Your investigation ends here."

"And your breach?"

"Contained, for now."

She paused at the door. "Be careful, Inspector. There are others asking questions. Others less interested in grain than in your health."

She adjusted her veil and left.
