# THE CAVE

*Placement: third meeting period (~1698-99). After the Go Home scene has failed. After the handkerchief. After Soonyi. Yi Hwon is unattached. They are undercover and almost caught.*

---

The footsteps passed within ten paces of the entrance.

They were pressed against the back wall of a shallow cave — barely a cave, more a fold in the rock face where the hillside had cracked and not bothered to close. He had pulled her in when the voices came up the trail. His hand on her arm, firm, operational — the grip of a man trained to move people out of danger without asking permission first.

They stood in the dark. His hand on her arm. Her back against the cold stone. The voices outside — two men, maybe three, speaking in the clipped register of officials conducting a search. Close enough that she could hear the creak of a leather satchel and the scrape of a boot on loose rock.

She held her breath. He held his. The cave smelled of wet stone and pine and something mineral, like a well that had dried.

The footsteps continued up the trail. The voices thinned. The creak of the satchel faded into the trees. Silence returned — not the comfortable kind but the held kind, the kind that waits for permission to become something else.

The danger was gone.

His hand was still on her arm.

---

She was aware of it. The weight of his hand. The fact that the operational reason for the contact had ended and the contact had not.

She had thought there would be more time with the husband. Five years and then nothing.

---

He looked at her.

She met his gaze. Calmly. The composure was real — it was always real — but underneath it something had shifted. The bricks were coming out of the wall. Not falling. Being removed. One by one. By her.

Her attention moved from his eyes to his neck. A thin red scratch ran along the side, just below his jaw — a branch, probably, from the scramble up the hillside. It was shallow. Already drying. The kind of small wound a man doesn't notice on himself.

"Your neck," she said.

He touched it with his free hand — the other still on her arm. He felt the scratch and looked at the blood on his fingertips.

She reached up with both hands.

Both. Not one hand being careful. Both hands committing.

She touched the scratch. Her fingers along the line of it, wiping what was left of the blood. The gesture was practical. It could have been nothing — a woman tending a minor wound in a dark cave after a close call. It could have been that.

Then she kissed it. Slowly. Her lips on the scratch, on the skin beside the scratch, on the place where his neck met his jaw. She stayed there. She did not rush.

He was frozen.

Her lips moved up. Slowly. Along his jaw. She reached his chin and paused — not hesitating, resting. She felt the roughness of his beard under her lips. Not a smooth jaw from a romance. The end of a day of hiding, running, being operational. She stayed with the roughness. She did not flinch from the reality of him.

Then she reached his mouth.

By that time, his hands had moved without his permission — the same way the hand had stayed on her arm after the danger passed. His body decided before his mind did. Both arms around her. He pulled her toward him. No more distance.

The thing she had maintained for months — rebuilt after every crack, defended with instructions and professional voices and morning light — was gone. Not broken. Released.

---

The kiss held.

She noticed herself not noticing.

The cave was cold. He was warm.

She stopped listening.
