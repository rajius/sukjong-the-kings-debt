# GO HOME

*Placement TBD — during the mission, before Yeongwol. Lady Yoon sends Yi Hwon back to Soonyi. Timing note: check whether Soonyi confrontation and marriage have already happened by this point.*

---

She chose the morning. Evenings were dangerous — low light, long silences, the kind of warmth that made careful people careless. Important things were said when the mind was cold and the light was honest.

He was packing his travel bundle by the door of the inn. She sat on the raised wooden floor of the corridor, her feet just above the courtyard stones.

"Yi Hwon."

He looked up. She almost never used his name.

"When this is finished," she said, "go home."

"I'll have to report to the capital first —"

"I don't mean the capital."

The courtyard was quiet. A rooster. The innkeeper's wife sweeping.

"The girl who made the handkerchief," she said. "Go back to her."

---

He set the bundle down.

"Lady Yoon —"

"I will not be the reason you set that aside."

Her voice was steady. Not cold. Precise — the way people are precise when they have thought about something for a long time and decided the only honest thing is to say it plainly.

"You are not as subtle as you think," she said. "And I am not as blind as you need me to be."

He opened his mouth. She raised one hand — barely, just the fingers lifting from her lap.

"What if I don't want —"

"That," she said, "is exactly why you need to."

---

She stood. She picked up her travel bundle and settled it on her shoulder with the practiced efficiency of a woman who had been carrying her own weight for years.

"We leave in an hour," she said. "The road to Yeongwol crosses the ridge at Baegun Pass."

She walked to the end of the corridor and turned the corner. Her footsteps on the wooden floor were even, unhurried, precise.

---

That night, at the waystation before the pass, she reported the day's observations as though nothing had happened. Road conditions. The state of the bridge. A rumor about the Yeongwol magistrate's deputy. Her voice was level, professional.

He listened. He took notes.

When she handed him the water gourd at the end of the evening, her fingers stayed a moment longer than they needed to.
