# Chapter 13 -- The Dead Man

Inspector Yu's second order arrived in the spring. The first assessment had been useful but incomplete. Yu wanted the ground verified: had the new appointments settled, were the provinces recovering or still frozen?

Yi Hwon went south again. The same roads, the same inns, the same clothes that did not announce him.

Jang was a side thread. A former clerk in a provincial dispatch office whose spending had begun to exceed his salary. Yi Hwon had been tracking him for three weeks when the trail led to a residence at the end of a lane that did not appear on any official map.

---

The door was open.

Yi Hwon stopped in the lane and listened. No voices. No movement. He waited for twenty breaths, then moved to the doorway.

The room had been searched — not ransacked, but searched by someone who knew what they were looking for. A writing desk opened, its drawers pulled out and pushed back, not quite aligned. A document case on the floor, empty. The brazier cold, with the edges of recently burned paper in the ash.

Jang lay near the desk, face down, one arm reaching toward the document case.

Yi Hwon crouched by the body. Dead for hours. No visible wound. No blood.

He was reading the scene when he felt the displacement of air behind him and to the left. Another body, close enough to touch. Close enough to have been there since before he entered.

The search had been specific — three drawers opened, the three that held correspondence. The lining of Jang's travel coat checked, his other clothes ignored. Papers burned, personal effects untouched. Someone who knew what Jang carried and where he kept it.

He started to turn.

She moved.

---

His arm locked behind him. Her forearm across his throat — placed, not crushing. The more he resisted, the tighter the lock would become.

He held still. He could feel the control in her arms — leverage, not strength. The forearm placement was too precise for improvisation. Two inches higher would have ended the conversation.

---

The veil loosened in the struggle. She felt it — the slackening cloth against her cheek.

She should have tightened it.

A draft through the doorway. The fabric lifted, settled, slipped free.

He saw part of her face first. The jaw. A cheekbone. One eye.

Then the veil fell.

He went very still.

The courtyard. The tea table. The gaze that held a beat too long. The morning she had not returned. A year of carrying that image, and the image was here, pressed against his back, with her forearm across his throat.

She felt the change in him. The tension had altered, no longer gathered for escape.

She felt the change in herself. Something in her arms that was not the hold. Something that had no operational name.

She stepped back at once. Not because of what he might betray. Because of what she could not account for in herself.

---

"Turn around."

He turned. She was at the far wall. Hands empty. The veil retied — hastily, the knot different from before.

He looked at her. She looked at him. Jang lay between them on the floor.

He did not say anything. He waited.

She studied him — closer than the memorial, closer than across a courtyard.

"You came here for the clerk," she said.

"Yes."

"You are too late."

"I can see that."

"Can you." She looked at the body, then back at him. "What do you see?"

"The desk was searched by someone who knew what they were looking for. The document case was opened from the inside — he gave them what they wanted before he died. The papers were burned one piece at a time. No visible wound." He paused. "And you are still here. Which means you arrived after whoever killed him, and you are looking for what they did not find."

She was quiet for a moment.

"Or," she said, "I arrived before."

Neither of them moved.

---

She moved toward the door.

"Your investigation ends here."

"Does it."

"Your report on this man does not enter the record. He died of an illness. You did not see me. You did not see this room."

"And in exchange?"

She stopped at the threshold.

"I will find you," she said. "When I am ready."

She adjusted the veil. She walked through the door without looking back.

He closed the door. He walked back up the lane.

He did not file a report that night. He sat in his room at the inn and looked at his hands and could still feel where the hold had been.
