# THE MEETING

*Placement: 1695, several days after "The Seowon." Lady Yoon controls the encounter this time.*

---

She had chosen the abandoned mill by the river. Far enough from town to be private, close enough to water that approaching footsteps would be heard clearly. Two escape routes, good sight lines, and the kind of place where a royal inspector would be distinctly uncomfortable.

Yi Hwon arrived exactly when she had specified. She watched him from the treeline as he approached - noting how he moved differently here than at the seowon. Less confident, more alert. Good.

"You're punctual," she said, stepping into the clearing.

"You said to come alone."

"And yet you brought backup." She gestured toward the ridge. "Two men. Well-hidden, but not well enough."

He didn't deny it. "Professional caution."

"Of course." She moved to the mill wheel, putting the structure between them. Her territory now. "You said someone else is hunting networks like mine."

"Yes. Not government. Something else."

"Who?"

"That's what I need to find out." He stepped closer to the mill. "Your dead contact - what else did he tell you before you killed him?"

"That he had been approached three times. Different people, same questions. They knew about supply routes, safe houses, how messages moved." She paused. "They knew about you."

"Me?"

"Asked specifically about a royal inspector asking questions in the provinces. Someone who didn't fit the usual pattern."

Yi Hwon was quiet for a moment. "What did he tell them?"

"Nothing, according to him. But he was compromised. Someone got to him."

"Do you believe that?"

"I believe he told me what he thought I wanted to hear." She stepped closer to the wheel. "The question is what he told them about me."

"And what do you think he told them?"

"Enough to be dangerous. Not enough to destroy everything." She looked at him directly. "Yet."

The word hung between them. He understood - her network was wounded, not dead, but time was running out.

"I can help you identify who's asking questions," he said. "Track them through official channels."

"And what do you want in exchange?"

"Your information," he said. "And mine in return."

"Nothing else?"

He held her gaze. "There is something else. But not as part of the trade."

She waited.

"The network exchange is straightforward. We meet twice more - you tell me what your contact revealed, I tell you what I discover about who's hunting your people. After that, officially, I found nothing worth pursuing."

"And unofficially?"

"You know very well I did not come only for your network."

She went very still.

"You are too young for a yangban widow."

"Then it is well that I am not speaking as a matchmaker."

She stared at him across the mill wheel. The confession hung between them, separate from their professional arrangement but impossible to ignore.

"And if I refuse?"

"Then you refuse." He stepped back slightly. "The work remains. That part was always real."

She found herself studying his face - the careful way he had separated his official investigation from his personal interest, the fact that he was not using one to pressure the other.

"Two more meetings," she said finally. "To exchange information about the network threat."

"Yes."

"And the other matter?"

"Remains separate. Your choice entirely."

She nodded once. "Then we understand each other."

"I believe we do."
