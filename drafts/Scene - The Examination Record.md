# THE EXAMINATION RECORD

*Placement: 1698 working-relationship sequence — after "Difficult" and "The Handkerchief." Yi Hwon discovers who the husband actually was.*

---

Yi Hwon told the archive clerk he was reviewing historical examination submissions. The clerk lost interest before the sentence was finished.

Something about Lady Yoon's husband had never fit its frame. The intelligence she carried — the speed, the capital-grade analysis applied to provincial information — none of this was learned in a country seowon.

He pulled the munkwa records.

---

He found him in the second volume. First-attempt pass. Song Si-yeol's student.

Yi Hwon's hand stopped on the page. He had sat for the munkwa himself. He knew what a first-attempt pass meant.

He read the submission. The essay title was written in precise calligraphy: "There is no arrangement by which correction may be made free of cost."

Most candidates hedged. They balanced perspectives to protect themselves from factional blowback.

Lady Yoon's husband had not hedged. Every sentence carried weight. He argued that rulers who promised to restore justice without acknowledging its price deceived both themselves and their subjects. That institutional wounds, once opened, could not be healed without creating new injuries elsewhere. That the wise ruler's task was not to avoid this cost, but to choose carefully which damage would be borne by whom.

He cited the Spring and Autumn Annals with the kind of close reading that suggested he had argued with them and won.

They passed him. First attempt.

---

Yi Hwon closed the volume.

A first-attempt munkwa pass. Song Si-yeol's student. Governance essays written with a clarity that left no room for misreading. After Song Si-yeol's execution, the apparatus would not have waited. The examination record itself was the evidence. Everything after was process.

The husband had been targeted long before he was killed.

Yi Hwon walked out into the daylight. He thought of Lady Yoon sitting on a rock in the sun, smiling.

*Difficult.*

She had come from that world. The capital. Song Si-yeol's circle. And after it destroyed her husband, she had taken everything she learned there and carried it to a place no one would think to watch.
