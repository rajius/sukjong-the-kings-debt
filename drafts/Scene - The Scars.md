# THE SCARS

*Placement: Late 1698, after "The Cave" and physical intimacy is established. Lady Yoon discovers Yi Hwon's past as the Busan port inspector and realizes how easily intimacy can become a way of extracting truth.*

---

She had not paid attention before. But his bare chest now showed marks from old wounds. Small ones, mostly healed white lines. She traced one gently with her finger, thinking.

He went still under her touch, then drew her closer, taking her curiosity for something else. She could have corrected him. She chose not to.

His breathing changed against her hair. The warmth of him pressed closer. Perhaps he had not mistaken anything at all.

---

Later, she asked about the wounds.

"Mostly from my military training." He shifted, showing her a scar along his thigh. "But this one was different. A knife fight in Busan. Over grain prices."

She studied the mark. He had positioned himself so she could see it clearly - whether this was simple explanation or something else, she could not tell. But the scar itself told a different story. It was long, deep enough to have left a permanent ridge. She had heard of such wounds.

"Are you sure this is about defending a farmer?"

He went very still.

She traced the scar with one finger. "Grain merchants do not usually fight with blades that precise."

"You know about wounds."

"I know about lies." She moved closer to him. "Who were you really fighting?"

He watched her face. She could not tell if the intensity in his eyes was from her touch or her question. 

"Lady Yoon—"

"Someone who knew how to handle a blade."

He reached for her, trying to pull her closer. She caught his hand and held it still. "Finish the story first."

"You know there are things I can't share."

"I know." 

He tried to reach her again. She leaned back and moved to get off the bed. 

He caught her waist, pulling her back. The quickness of it told her enough.

She settled against him. "The Busan port investigation. Three years ago."

He was very still.

"My network heard about it. An inspector who got too close to the grain merchants." She traced the scar again. "I heard someone almost died."

His breathing had changed.

She looked up at him. "I always wondered what happened to that inspector."

She could feel his pulse under her palm. Recognition was enough. She did not need confession.

---

Later that night, she lay with her eyes closed, thinking. Yi Hwon had fallen asleep beside her.

He would always be surrounded by danger. Men with knives in port cities. Officials who would rather lose an inspector than an arrangement. The kind of work that left scars like the one she had traced tonight.

She could lose him at any time. To a blade, to a mission, to the same machinery that had taken her husband. 

She opened her eyes and looked at him in the darkness. His breathing was even, his face relaxed in sleep.

He was here. That was all.

Not safe. Not promised. Only here.

She let that be enough.
