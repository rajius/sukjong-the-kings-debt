# THE SCARS

*Placement: Late 1698, after "The Cave" and physical intimacy is established. Lady Yoon discovers Yi Hwon's past as the Busan port inspector and learns to use intimacy as interrogation.*

---

She had not paid attention before. But his bare chest now showed marks from old wounds. Small ones, mostly healed white lines. She traced one gently with her finger, thinking.

He went still under her touch, then drew her closer. She realized he had mistaken her curiosity for something else. She could have corrected him. She chose not to.

His breathing changed against her hair. The warmth of him pressed closer. Perhaps he had not mistaken anything at all.

She let him believe what he needed to believe.

---

Later, she asked about the wounds.

"Mostly from my military training." He shifted, showing her a scar along his thigh. "But this one was different. A knife fight in Busan. Over grain prices."

She studied the mark. He had positioned himself so she could see it clearly - whether this was simple explanation or something else, she could not tell. But the scar itself told a different story. It was long, deep enough to have left a permanent ridge. She had heard of such wounds.

"Are you sure this is about defending a farmer?"

He went very still.

She traced the scar with one finger. The intimacy of the gesture did not soften her voice. "Grain merchants do not usually fight with blades that precise. And farmers do not usually have disputes worth dying for."

"You know about wounds."

"I know about lies." She kept her finger on the scar, then moved closer to him. She had never used intimacy this way before, but she had watched other women do it. Had studied how they made proximity into pressure, warmth into weakness.

"Who were you really fighting?"

He watched her face, and she could not tell if the intensity in his eyes was from her touch or her question. Perhaps he did not know either.

She let her hand rest against his leg. "You can tell me the truth, you know." The words felt strange in her mouth - softer than her usual interrogations, but no less deliberate.

"Lady Yoon—" His voice had changed, less controlled than usual.

"Was it a government official? Someone taking bribes from the merchants?" Her voice stayed gentle even as her questions grew sharper. She leaned against him, testing this new territory. "Someone who knew how to handle a blade?"

He tried to shift away slightly, but she followed the movement. Then he reached for her, trying to pull her closer, to change what was happening between them back to something he understood.

She caught his hand and held it still. "Finish the story first."

Whatever game he had started by showing her the scar, he was no longer controlling it.

"You know there are things I can't share."

"I know." 

He tried to reach her again. She leaned back calmly and decided to get off the bed. 

He reached her waist swiftly, putting her back into his lap. She smiled. His response revealed everything she needed to know.

She settled against him, no longer resisting. "The Busan port investigation. Three years ago."

His whole body went rigid.

"My network heard about it. An inspector who got too close to the grain merchants." She traced the scar again, gently. "I heard someone almost died. Was that you?"

His breathing had stopped entirely.

"The official report said the operation was suspended after the inspector was injured. But the corruption network was never fully dismantled." She looked up at him. "I always wondered what happened to that inspector."

His grip on her waist tightened.

"So when you say it was complicated," she continued quietly, "you mean it was an intelligence operation that nearly got you killed."

She could feel his pulse racing where her hand rested against his chest.

"Now I understand why you cannot share certain things."

---

Later that night, she lay with her eyes closed, thinking. Yi Hwon had fallen asleep beside her.

He would always be surrounded by danger. Men with knives in port cities. Officials who preferred dead inspectors to honest reports. The kind of work that left scars like the one she had traced tonight.

She could lose him at any time. To a blade, to a mission, to the same machinery that had taken her husband. 

But when he was here, like this, she felt something she had almost forgotten. Peace. Not the careful quiet of her network operations, not the controlled stillness of her public face. Something deeper.

She opened her eyes and looked at him in the darkness. His breathing was even, his face relaxed in sleep. For these few hours, he was not an inspector carrying sealed orders. He was not a man marked by dangerous work.

He was simply here. With her. Safe.

She closed her eyes again and let herself have this feeling, knowing it was temporary, knowing it was dangerous in its own way. But real, for now.
